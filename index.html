The paragraph below states the goal of the experiment:

The goal of the experiment was to design a procedure to seperate a mixture of salt, sand, iron, and water while preserving the most amount of each component. The desgin process started with us planning out how could seperate the mixture into two seperate mixtures with two components each. We decided the best way to do this was to pass it through a coffee filter. The filter would let the now disolved salt and water through while keeping the sand and iron from going through. Seperating the iron and sand would be easy enough with a magnet but the saltwater would a lot harder to . The salt being disolved into the water made it so we couldn't just use a sieve or a magnet to filter it out. We finally discovered that boiling the water would cause it to evaporate leaving the salt alone. We then collected the water as it condensed onto plastic wrap and trickled down into a container.

Procedure:

Water Separation Setup-

  1. place a metal chassis under a flask (flask A) and place another flask next to it on base elevation (flask B).
  2. connect one end of the tubing to flask B

Seperating-

  1. pour the entire solution through a coffee filter into the beaker. You now have a mixture (in coffee filter) and a solution (in beaker).
  2. run a magnet through the mixture of solids to separate the iron from the sand
  3. scrap off the iron shavings into another coffee filter as a container
  4. take the salt and water solution and pour it into flask A and put the other end of the tubing on flask A, completing the connection
  5. add enough heat to boil or almost boil the water and wait until all the water has been separated from the salt and the water has condensed and dropped into flask B
  6. remove the tube connecting the flasks and pour the salt into a coffee filter (you may need to scrap it off the flask with a tool)
  7. you now have 3 coffee filters with solids in them and flask B has water. Weigh and get results
